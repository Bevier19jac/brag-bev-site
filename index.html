Dumpster Diver FAQ

Six quick answers to the questions operators and facilities teams ask most often about Dumpster Diver.

What is Dumpster Diver designed to clean? + −

Dumpster Diver is purpose-built for high-impact exterior cleanup—dumpster pads, loading zones, compactors, and adjacent hard surfaces where grease, residue, and odor build up quickly.

Is Dumpster Diver safe for food-adjacent areas? + −

Final chemistry and labeling will determine specific claims, but Dumpster Diver is being developed with food-adjacent standards in mind so it can be used confidently around back-of-house and waste-handling zones.

How often should teams use Dumpster Diver? + −

Most sites will run Dumpster Diver on a set cadence—for example weekly or after heavy service days—so the pad never reaches the point where it feels out of control.

Can Dumpster Diver be used with pressure washers? + −

Yes—the system is being designed to play nicely with the pressure-washing hardware many operators already own, while tightening up how product is applied and rinsed.

Does Dumpster Diver require special storage? + −

Storage requirements will follow the final chemistry and regulatory guidance, but the intent is a compact footprint that lives neatly with existing janitorial or exterior-maintenance supplies.

How does Dumpster Diver support sustainability goals? + −

The system is being designed to reduce wasteful over-application and unnecessary rework, so teams use fewer resources to keep pads clean and compliant while maintaining a premium guest experience.
